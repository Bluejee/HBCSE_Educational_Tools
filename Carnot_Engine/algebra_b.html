The word done by the gas in expanding or Compressing isothermally at a temperature $T$ from a volume of $V_a$ to $V_b$ is given by,

$$W = nR~T~ln\frac{V_b}{V_a}$$

As there is no change in internal energy of the system, this work is equal to the heat absorbed in this stage. i.e. $Q = W$

For Adiabatic Expansion or Compression, No heat is supplied. But work is done.

The main question we try to answer is the ratio of the fraction of enery supplied from the hot reservoir that is turned into mechanical work.

Since the internal energy of the gas is the same at the begining and the end,

i.e. efficiency, $$\eta = \frac{Q_H -Q_C}{Q_H} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}$$

Work done by the gas in a small volume expansion of $\Delta V$ is $P~\Delta V$

hence the work done in expanding isothermally at a temprature $T$ from a volume of $V_a$ to $V_b$ is the area under the curve given by the integral,

$$W = \int_{V_a}^{V_b}PdV = \int_{V_a}^{V_b}\frac{nRT}{V}dV = nRT~ln\frac{V_b}{V_a}$$

We now have the expressions for $Q_H$ and $Q_C$ given as,

$$Q_H = nRT_H~ln\frac{V_b}{V_a}$$ $$Q_C = nRT_C~ln\frac{V_c}{V_d}$$

In this $a \rightarrow b$ is expansion and $c \rightarrow d$ is compression, hence the work done would be negetive. Hence, we interchange the neumerator and denominator.

From the equation for Adiabatic Processes,

$$PV^\gamma = Constant$$ i.e.
$$T_HV_b^{\gamma-1} = T_CV_c^{\gamma-1} $$ $$T_HV_a^{\gamma-1} = T_CV_d^{\gamma-1} $$ From this, we get,
$$\frac{V_b}{V_a} = \frac{V_c}{V_d}$$

Using these, we can solve for the relationship between $Q$ and $T$ as,

$$\frac{Q_H}{T_H} = \frac{Q_C}{T_C}$$

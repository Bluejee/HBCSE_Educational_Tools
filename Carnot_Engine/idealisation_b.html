Creating The Ideal Heat Engine

  • We have previously concluded that the ideal heat engine would have a piston that moves between hot and cold sources, eliminating the irreversibility encountered during combustion.
  • In addition to the piston movement, other processes also take place within the system.
  • To achieve the most ideal and efficient heat engine, it is essential that all processes occurring within the system are reversible.
  • To ensure that our system is indeed ideal and to uncover any non-ideal processes, let us solve some questions related to the system's behavior and efficiency.
  • There is a mechanical process that converts mechanical energy into thermal energy, which cannot be fully regained. Can you identify this process and suggest a solution to overcome this issue?
  • Heat exchange is necessary for the gas in the piston to expand and contract in-order for the engine to do work. However, not all processes of heat exchange are reversible. For maximum efficiency, it is necessary that the process be reversible. Can you suggest a solution to ensure reversibility in the heat exchange process?
  • The isothermal expansion and compression of the gas in the piston in the Carnot cycle takes place at the temperature of the heat source and sink. In order to achieve maximum efficiency, it is necessary to match the temperature of the gas in the piston with the temperature of the heat source or sink. What is the process for changing the temperature of the gas in the piston in a reversible manner? Can you suggest a solution that ensures maximum efficiency in the Carnot cycle?
  • Friction!
    Friction can cause irreversibility in the cycle and reduce the efficiency of the engine. Therefore, having a piston without friction is essential for the Carnot engine to achieve the maximum efficiency possible.
  • A solution to ensure reversibility in the heat exchange process is to perform the heat exchange isothermally. This means that the process should happen slowly enough to maintain a constant temperature, so that the heat exchange is reversible. for this we use heat sources and sinks that can provide infinite energy and ensure that the heat baths are maintained at constant tempratures.
  • To change the temperature of the gas in the piston in a reversible manner, an adiabatic process must be used. An adiabatic process is a process in which there is no heat transfer between the system and its surroundings. This can be achieved by having a thermally insulated piston placed on an insulating stand, so that the heat transfer between the gas and the surroundings is minimized.

Gerat!
We now have an Ideal Heat Engine. This is called the Carnot Engine.
The Carnot engine is a theoretical model that represents an ideal heat engine. It is made up of a frictionless piston that moves between two heat baths at different temperatures. The heat baths provide infinite energy, and their temperatures are maintained constant. The piston is perfectly insulated from the environment and has a perfectly conducting base. The engine operates in a thermally isolated system, with no energy exchange with the surroundings. The Carnot engine operates through a series of reversible isothermal and adiabatic processes, allowing it to achieve the maximum possible efficiency.
